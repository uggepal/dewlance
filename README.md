## A Little Background

Dewlance was founded in February 2009 by Chandra Prakash, running out of Delhi, India. It's a lean operation — reportedly six people — which explains both the extremely low prices and the occasionally sluggish ticket response times that some users have flagged. Over 16+ years they've built a customer base in the thousands, maintained a 4.9/5 rating from their own testimonials, and earned a 6.2/10 on WHTop from 13 community reviews — with 8 recommending and 5 opposing. Not a perfect score, but for a host this cheap, it's actually respectable.

The core pitch is simple: AMD EPYC processors, NVMe storage, multiple data center locations, and rock-bottom prices. No late fees on overdue invoices, either — a genuinely unusual policy in an industry that loves to pile on penalties.

**The prices.** There's no other way to say it. $6.50/year for NVMe shared hosting with free SSL, DirectAdmin, and Softaculous is remarkable. Most hosts charge that much for a month, and then double it on renewal.

**No overdue fees.** This is a small thing that matters a lot when you're juggling multiple projects and subscriptions. Dewlance explicitly does not charge late fees on overdue invoices.

**Free migration.** Switching hosts is a pain. Dewlance handles it for free, which removes one of the main friction points in moving.

**Server location choice.** US, UK, and Australia for shared hosting. Picking a server closer to your audience genuinely matters for load speed, and having three continents covered is better than most budget hosts manage.

**AMD EPYC + NVMe across the board.** This isn't old spinning-disk infrastructure. The hardware is current.

## What to Keep in Mind

Support ticket response times are a recurring theme in reviews — the average is described as 18 minutes and 34 seconds on their own site, but some users report waits of 1–6 hours, which is on the slower end for urgent issues. For a small team of six people running a global operation, this is somewhat expected. If you need enterprise-level SLA guarantees, this isn't the host for that.

Storage limits on shared plans are real. The Arya plan's 3GB NVMe is plenty for most blogs and portfolio sites, but if you're running a media-heavy site or storing lots of files, you'll want to size up or look at the VPS options.

The platform is more "utilitarian" than polished — DirectAdmin rather than cPanel on most plans, no flashy dashboard, no marketing fluff. For users who know their way around a control panel, this is perfectly fine. For total beginners expecting a lot of hand-holding, the learning curve is slightly steeper.
